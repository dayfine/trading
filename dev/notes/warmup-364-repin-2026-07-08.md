## Findings — the floor-brake/meme-squeeze pathology (feeds P1b)

The RS-honest basis surfaced a real risk-control defect on
`sp500-2010-2026` (long-only):

1. RS present from the first screen → the screener faithfully catches **GME's
   Sept-2020 Stage-2 breakout** (entry 2020-09-12 @ $6.26, 74,069 sh). Book
   says ride it: exit 2021-02-03 @ $111.95 = **+$7.83M realized** on one name.
2. The Jan-2021 squeeze marks NAV to a $28.9M MTM peak.
   `Force_liquidation.Peak_tracker` is **monotonic**; the floor is
   `NAV < 0.4 × peak`. GME's collapse drops NAV to $9.9M → floor fires
   2021-02-02 → all positions liquidated + entry halt.
3. NAV (~$11.5M) never re-clears 0.4 × $28.9M = $11.57M, and the peak never
   decays → **the run is sterilized for its remaining 5 years** (32
   Portfolio_floor liqs; ~zero entries 2021/2023/2024; OPV 0 at end; flat
   equity for months at a time). The longshort twin (hedged, no floor fire)
   rides the same window healthily: 362% / DD 21.3.

This is a **floor-quality-program (P1b) input**, not a warmup-fix defect: a
left-tail circuit breaker keyed to a monotonic MTM high-water mark is fragile
to squeeze-shaped spikes — one position's parabolic MTM (never realized) can
permanently disable the strategy. Candidate fixes belong to P1b's
circuit-breaker design space (decaying/realized-basis peak, floor reset on
re-entry conditions, per-position vs portfolio separation). The golden now
documents the artifact loudly (`goldens-sp500-historical/sp500-2010-2026.sexp`
pin comment).
