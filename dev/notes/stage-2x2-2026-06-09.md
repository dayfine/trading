## Verdict 1 — `enable_stage2_ma_hold`: REJECT

Despite collapsing the stage oscillation **visually** (KO 2010-13 → one S2×160 on
the chart), the MA-hold **degrades the strategy** on every aggregate axis: Sharpe
0.643→0.486 (force_exit on) / 0.679→0.457 (off), lower Calmar + DSR, off the
frontier. **Visual stage-coherence ≠ better returns** — holding Stage 2 through
pullbacks keeps positions/eligibility that the strategy is better off rotating
out of. The chart diagnosis was real (the oscillation exists) but suppressing it
this way costs more than it saves. Stays default-off. *(Humbling but important:
chart intuition still has to clear WF-CV — the program's recurring lesson.)*

## Verdict 2 — `enable_stage3_force_exit=false`: INCONCLUSIVE-POSITIVE (most promising lever to date)

Disabling the Stage-3 force-exit — **deferring exits to the trailing stop** — is
the **sole Pareto-frontier cell**: best Sharpe (0.679 vs 0.643), best Calmar
(1.631 vs 1.382), lowest MaxDD (14.74), best DSR (0.9977 vs 0.9964). This
**confirms the chart-derived hypothesis**: the S3 force-exit fires on
whipsaw-prone topping flags (price still ~3-7% off the high, often still above
the MA), so exiting on it sells dips that mostly resume — the autopsy's #1
missed-gain mode. The stop is the better arbiter.

This is notable as **the first net-positive mechanism change on the broad
universe — and it is a *removal*** (turn a mechanism off), consistent with the
program's "breadth + simplicity, not more dials" thesis. It also likely explains
**why the 6 S3-exit-timing dials (hysteresis, exit-margin, late-stage2, …) all
got REJECTED** — they were tuning a fundamentally whipsaw-prone exit instead of
deleting it.

**But not yet promotable:** the edge is modest and the per-fold win-count is only
1/15 (the aggregate gain is concentrated, not robustly per-fold). Per
`.claude/rules/promotion-confirmation.md`, run a **confirmation grid** (≥3
period×universe contexts incl. a deep pre-2009 macro regime) before flipping the
default. Do NOT promote on this single surface.
