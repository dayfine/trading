# H3 result: skip_ad_breadth has negligible RSS effect — Promote_full is the smoking gun (2026-04-24)

## Hypothesis (from `dev/plans/backtest-perf-2026-04-24.md`)

> **H3**: AD-breadth load dominates upstream RSS at large universe sizes.
> Test: A/B with `--override '(skip_ad_breadth true)'`.
> Expected if true: RSS drops by AD-breadth slice; OOM avoided at scale.

## Setup

- Branch: `main` post-#538 (memtrace integration merged 2026-04-24T14:04:52Z)
- Tooling: `dev/scripts/run_perf_hypothesis.sh H3 ...` (the C2 harness from #537)
- Per-side artifacts produced: `*.peak_rss_kb`, `*.trace.sexp`, `*.memtrace.ctf`
- Scoped data dir: `/tmp/data-small-302` (292 symbols, same as PR #524 baseline)
- Scenario: `goldens-small/bull-crash-2015-2020.sexp` (6-year, 1510 trading days)
- Override applied to BOTH sides: `--override '((skip_ad_breadth true))'`
- Local 14-min run inside docker

## Result

| | Baseline (no override, PR #524) | H3 (skip_ad_breadth) | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legacy peak RSS | 1,871,240 KB | 1,879,620 KB | **+8,380 KB (+0.4%)** |
| Tiered peak RSS | 3,652,852 KB | 3,800,128 KB | **+147,276 KB (+4.0%)** |
| Tiered/Legacy ratio | 1.95× | 2.02× | barely moved |

**H3 NOT confirmed.** AD-breadth load is not a meaningful contributor to RSS for either Legacy or Tiered. Skipping it ACTUALLY INCREASED Tiered's RSS slightly (likely because the strategy's path-dependent decisions changed, allocating different intermediate state).

## Where Tiered's memory actually goes

Cumulative RSS by Tiered phase boundary (subtracting prior cumulative gives delta-allocated-during-phase):

- After `Load_universe`: ~22 MB (universe parse)
- After `Promote_metadata`: 668 MB → **~647 MB allocated during Metadata-promote**. 292 symbols × Metadata.t (last_close, sector, cap, 30d_avg_volume) at this size implies Metadata.t is far larger than its surface fields suggest, OR the loader is also retaining intermediate parse state (CSV buffers, hashmaps, etc.).
- After `Promote_full`: 3.74 GB → **~3.07 GB allocated during Full-promote**. 292 symbols × Full.t.bars (~250 daily bars each) = 73K bars. Even at 75 bytes/bar that's ~5 MB of bar data — but actual allocation is 600× more. Strong signal that `Full.t` carries significant retained state beyond bars (possibly the Summary scalar computation cache, OR Bar_history's seed-from-Full path retains the full bar lists in two places).
- After `Fill` / `Demote` / `Teardown`: stable at 3.74 GB (no further growth — the backtest loop itself doesn't add memory).

Legacy by contrast:
- After `Load_universe`: ~22 MB
- During `Fill` (bar-by-bar accumulate): grows to 1.88 GB
- That's the entire Legacy story — no Promote phases, just the bar accumulation pattern.

So Tiered's overhead vs Legacy isn't "we kept Bar_history bigger" — it's "we materialized 3 GB during the Promote phases that Legacy doesn't have at all". The RSS gap is in `Promote_metadata` + `Promote_full`, not in `Bar_history.accumulate`.
